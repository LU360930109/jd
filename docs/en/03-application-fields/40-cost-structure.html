Cost Structure

Definition

Proportion of various expenses in tape production.

Historical Background

1980s: cost management technology enabled precise accounting.

Example

Raw materials, labor, equipment depreciation, energy. Like an itemized bill.

Formula

Total Cost = Raw Materials + Labor + Equipment Depreciation + Energy + Overhead

Core Concepts

Profit analysis foundation: raw materials typically 50-70% of total cost.

Extended Applications

Raw materials: 50-70%; labor: 10-15%; equipment: 5-10%; energy: 5-8%; overhead: 5-10%; sales: 3-5%; finance: 2-3%.

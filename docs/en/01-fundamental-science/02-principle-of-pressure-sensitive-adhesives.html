Principle of Pressure-Sensitive Adhesives

Details

Pressure-sensitive adhesives (PSA) are materials that are tacky at room temperature and can bond with just light pressure. Their working principle is based on viscoelasticity — the adhesive layer deforms under external force, increasing contact area with the substrate, while adhesive molecules generate intermolecular forces such as Van der Waals forces and hydrogen bonds.

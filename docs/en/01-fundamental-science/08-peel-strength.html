Peel Strength

Definition

Force required to peel tape from the adherend at a specified angle.

Historical Background

1950s: 90-degree peel test became industry standard.

Example

Easy detachment = low peel strength; tearing paper fibers = high. Like a tug-of-war.

Formula

Peel Strength = Maximum Peel Force (N) / Tape Width (mm)

Core Concepts

Ideally cohesive failure (within adhesive) rather than adhesive failure (separation from substrate).

Extended Applications

Packaging: 3-8 N/25mm; automotive paint protection: 15-25; industrial: 50-100; medical: 1-3.

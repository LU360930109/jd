Thermal Conductivity

Definition

Ability to transfer heat, key for heat-dissipating tapes.

Historical Background

1990s: thermally conductive tapes replaced bulky metal conductors.

Example

Thermally conductive tape between CPU and heat sink rapidly transfers heat.

Formula

Thermal Conductivity (W/m.K) = Heat Flow x Thickness / Area x Temp Diff

Core Concepts

Phonon conduction through molecular chains. Fillers enhance it.

Extended Applications

Ordinary: 0.2; conductive: 1-3; high: 5-10; ultra-high: 20-30 (5G); graphene: 100+ W/m.K.

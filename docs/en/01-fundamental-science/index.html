Fundamental Science

Scientific Foundation of the Adhesive Tape Industry

This section covers the core scientific knowledge of the adhesive tape industry, including adhesive chemistry, materials science, and physical property testing. Understanding these concepts is the first step to mastering the industry.

01

Definition and Classification of Adhesives

Understanding the basic concepts, classification methods, and application scenarios of adhesives is fundamental to the adhesive tape industry.

02

Principle of Pressure-Sensitive Adhesives

Pressure-sensitive adhesives are the core technology of the adhesive tape industry, and understanding their working principle is crucial for product development.

03

Base Materials

Base materials are the skeleton of adhesive tape, determining its physical properties and application scenarios.

04

Coating Process

The coating process is the core link in adhesive tape manufacturing, determining the quality and performance of the tape.

05

Release Agents

Release agents are isolation coatings on the tape surface that prevent self-adhesion of the adhesive layer.

06

Tack

Tack is the bonding force generated instantly when the tape is lightly pressed against the substrate.

07

Hold Power

Hold power is the ability of the tape to maintain its bonding state under continuous load.

08

Peel Strength

Peel strength is the force required to peel the tape from the substrate surface.

09

Tape Thickness

Tape thickness affects the flexibility, strength, and user experience of the tape.

10

Tape Width

Tape width is an important specification parameter that affects application scenarios and efficiency.

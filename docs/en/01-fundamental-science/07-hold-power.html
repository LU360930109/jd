Hold Power

Definition

Ability to maintain bonded state under sustained load, reflecting lasting endurance.

Historical Background

1980s: constant-temperature/constant-load instruments standardized evaluation.

Example

Wall hook holding 24 hours = good holding power. Like a marathon runner: endurance, not explosive strength.

Formula

Holding Power = Load (N) / Bonding Area (mm2) x Time (h)

Core Concepts

Creep resistance: molecules resisting flow under sustained force, depending on crosslinking and cohesive strength.

Extended Applications

Pipeline: >10,000 hours; automotive structural: 30-year vibration; electronic: -40C to 150C cycling; curtain wall: 15-year wind.

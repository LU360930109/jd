Chemical Resistance

Definition

Ability to resist degradation from acids, alkalis, solvents, and oils.

Historical Background

1970s: fluoroelastomers and silicones expanded chemical adaptability.

Example

Lab tape contacts alcohol/acetone. Ordinary dissolves; resistant stays intact.

Formula

Chemical Resistance = f(Polymer Type, Crosslink Density, Additives)

Core Concepts

Molecular structural stability: C-F, Si-O bonds are key for resistance.

Extended Applications

Acid: chemical plants; alkali: paper mills; solvent: labs; oil: auto factories.

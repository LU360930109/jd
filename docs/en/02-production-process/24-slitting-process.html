Slitting Process

Definition

Cutting large master rolls into smaller rolls or specific widths.

Historical Background

1960s: high-speed slitting machines enabled any-width cutting.

Example

1,000 mm master rolls slit to 48 mm, 72 mm. Like cutting cake.

Formula

Number of Slits = Master Roll Width / Finished Product Width

Core Concepts

Customization requiring clean edges, no overflow, no delamination.

Extended Applications

Narrow: 1-6 mm (electronic); standard: 10-100 mm; wide: 100-500 mm; precision: +/-0.1 mm (optical).
